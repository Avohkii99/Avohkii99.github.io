About the National Water Model

The National Water Model (NWM) is a hydrologic model that simulates and forecasts water movement across the continental United States. It provides high-resolution forecasts of streamflow and other hydrologic variables, which are essential for water resource management, flood prediction, and environmental protection. The NWM leverages data from various sources, including weather forecasts, land surface models, and observations, to provide accurate and timely water forecasts.

How does it work?

This page allows users to retrieve US National Water Model short-range forecasts for a specific reach ID. Users can enter a reach ID in the input box and click the "Get Forecast" button. The application will then fetch the forecast data for the specified reach ID, display the values in a table, and draw the forecast in a graph using a custom script.
